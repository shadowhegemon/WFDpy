Winter Field Day 2026 - Official Rules

Complete rules and guidelines from the Winter Field Day Association

Event Overview

When:

January 24-25, 2026 (Last full weekend in January)

Operational Period: 30 hours from 1600 UTC Saturday (11 AM EST) to 2159 UTC Sunday (4:59 PM EST)

Objective:

WFD is a multi-objective event. The primary objective is to set up an amateur radio field station and successfully make multiple contacts. This is an Operating Event, not a contest - it's about emergency preparedness and skills development.

Setup Time:

Stations may begin setup no earlier than 1600 UTC Friday, with cumulative setup time not exceeding 12 hours.

Location Constraint: All stations under a single call sign must be within a circular area of 1,000 feet (300m) diameter.

Exchange Format

Exchange: Call Sign + Category Number + Class Identifier + Location Identifier
Category (Number):

Number of transceivers capable of transmitting simultaneously. Examples: 1, 2, 3, etc.

Class Identifiers:
  • H Home: Inside a permanent livable residence
  • I Indoor: Away from home, inside insulated building on permanent foundation (plumbing, heating/cooling)
  • O Outdoor: Partly or fully exposed building/shelter without typical utilities
  • M Mobile/Mobile Stationary: RV, car, van, trailer, etc. (must have ability to be mobile)
Location Identifier:

US/Canadian stations use ARRL/RAC Section. Mexico stations use "MX". All others use "DX".

Exchange Examples:
  • 2M EPA - Two mobile stations in East Pennsylvania
  • 1H GA - One home station in Georgia
  • 4O WTX - Four outdoor stations in West Texas
  • 6I MN - Six indoor stations in Minnesota

Bands & Modes

Allowed Bands:

All amateur bands EXCEPT:

  • 12 meters
  • 17 meters
  • 30 meters
  • 60 meters
Power Limit:
Maximum 100 Watts PEP for all stations
Allowed Modes:

All modes EXCEPT WSJT modes

  • Phone: SSB, AM, FM, DMR, C4FM, etc.
  • CW: Morse code
  • Digital: PSK, RTTY, Olivia, Packet, SSTV, ATV, JS8Call, etc.
Prohibited: WSJT modes (FT4, FT8, JT65, etc.) - don't support emergency traffic effectively

Scoring System

QSO Points:
  • Phone contacts: 1 point each
  • CW and Digital contacts: 2 points each
Contact Rules:
  • Each station may be worked once per band-mode combination
  • Maximum 3 times per band (Phone, CW, Digital)
  • Example: K4FUN on 20m = 5 points total (1 Phone + 2 CW + 2 Digital)
Final Score Formula:
Total Score = (Total QSO Points) × (Objective Multiplier + 1)
Restrictions:
  • No repeater contacts allowed
  • No cross-band contacts (except satellites for objectives)
  • No internet-linked modes
  • Direct RF contacts only

Objectives & Multipliers

Objectives should be your primary focus during WFD!

Power & Operation (OM):
  • Alternative Power (×1): 100% non-grid power (batteries, solar, etc.)
  • Away from Home (×3): >0.5 miles from home
  • QRP Operation (×4): <10W phone, <5W CW/digital
  • 6 Continuous Hours (×2): Monitor radio for 6+ hours
Technical (OM):
  • Multiple Antennas (×1): Deploy 2+ antennas, 1+ contact each
  • Multiple Modes (×2): Use 2+ mode groups
  • Winlink Email (×1): Send/receive via RF
  • WFD Bulletin (×1): Copy special bulletin
Satellites (OM):
  • FM Satellite (×2): 1+ FM satellite contact
  • SSB/CW Satellite (×3): 1+ linear satellite contact

Note: Satellite contacts don't count toward QSO points or category number

Band Objectives (OM):
  • Six Bands (×6): 3+ contacts on 6+ different bands
  • Twelve Bands (×6): 3+ contacts on 12+ different bands
Multiplier Calculation: Each completed objective adds to your OM. Base multiplier is always OM+1.

Log Submission

Required Information:
  • Date and time (UTC) of each contact
  • Station worked (callsign)
  • Band and mode
  • Complete exchange sent and received
  • Points claimed for each contact
Submission Details:
  • Deadline: March 1, 2026 at 11:59 UTC
  • Format: Cabrillo or ADIF file
  • Website: www.winterfieldday.org
  • Certificate: Downloaded after submission
